Is a Hypervisor Safe or Risky?

The debate over hypervisors often boils down to a choice between maximum security and maximum performance. While hypervisors are inherently "safe"—they are designed to protect you—the "risk" lies in system stability and hardware overhead.

The Safety Benefits

By running your OS inside a virtualized container, common exploits are neutralized before they can reach your data. This is why Microsoft enables it by default in Windows 11.

The Performance Risks

On older or mid-range CPUs, the hypervisor can consume up to 15% of your processing power. If you are chasing 240Hz gaming, this "security blanket" might feel more like a lead weight.
