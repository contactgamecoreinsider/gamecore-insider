How to Enable 'Handheld Boost Mode' on Switch 2: A Performance Guide

Published: March 28, 2026 | By GameCore Staff

The Nintendo Switch 2 has finally introduced a feature fans have been begging for: Handheld Boost Mode. This allows the console to tap into extra clock speeds even when not docked, bringing portable performance closer to the "Mid-Tier PC" experience.

Steps to Activate Boost Mode

1. Navigate to System Settings from the Home Menu.
2. Scroll down to Power & Performance.
3. Select Handheld Optimization.
4. Toggle Boost Mode to 'On' (Note: This will decrease battery life by approximately 20%).

Is it Worth the Battery Drain?

For titles like Crimson Desert or Metroid Prime 4, the answer is a resounding yes. Boost Mode stabilizes framerates and pushes the internal resolution from 720p to a crisp 1080p via AI upscaling, making it an essential tweak for "pro" handheld gamers.
